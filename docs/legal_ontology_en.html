Ontology Reference

Legal Knowledge Base Ontology

A structured model for representing Brazilian legal knowledge — laws, provisions, decisions, cases, courts, parties, topics, and temporal validity — designed to support RAG and GraphRAG retrieval pipelines.

OWL / TTL GraphRAG Brazilian Law LangChain · MongoDBGraphStore English

1Purpose and Scope

This ontology models legal knowledge for a Brazilian-oriented legal knowledge base.

It represents laws, legal provisions, court cases, judicial decisions, parties, topics, and temporal validity so that legal information can be indexed, linked, and queried.

Goal 1 Entity normalization

Normalize legal entities and relationships for consistent representation across the knowledge base.

Goal 2 RAG / GraphRAG support

Support retrieval pipelines with explicit legal structure — enabling both vector and graph-based queries.

Goal 3 Legal reasoning

Enable reasoning over citations, applicability, and legal validity periods across linked sources.

2Core Classes

Thirteen classes represent the primary entities in the legal domain.

LegalSource

Generic legal source — norms, decisions, and doctrine-like references.

Norm

Normative act: constitution, law, decree, resolution, or ordinance.

Provision

Granular legal unit: article, paragraph, inciso, or alinea.

Decision

Judicial decision: sentence, ruling, or appellate decision.

Case

Judicial process identified by a case number.

Court

Judicial body — e.g. STF, STJ, TJSP.

Chamber

Panel or chamber inside a court.

Person / Organization

Basic legal actors appearing in cases and decisions.

Party

Litigating party in a case — either a person or an organization.

Lawyer

Person subclass representing legal counsel in a case.

Judge

Person subclass representing the presiding judge.

Topic

Legal taxonomy concept — e.g. Civil Liability, Moral Damages.

Jurisdiction

Territorial or legal scope — e.g. BR, SP.

TemporalValidity

Effective interval (effectiveFrom / effectiveTo) for legal sources.

6Structural Constraints

Cardinality rules keep the graph legally coherent and reduce data ambiguity.

Class Constraint Rationale
Provision Every Provision belongs to exactly 1 Norm. A legal article cannot exist independently of its parent statute.
Decision Every Decision decides at least 1 Case. A judicial ruling must reference the case it resolves.
Case Every Case is heard by at least 1 Court. A case without a jurisdictional court cannot be properly indexed.

8Why This Ontology Works Well for Graph RAG

🎯
Entity normalization

Standardised classes and identifiers improve retrieval precision by reducing ambiguity across documents.

🔗
Multi-hop reasoning

Explicit edges — cites, applies, decidesCase — allow queries that traverse multiple entity types.

📅
Temporal validity

The TemporalValidity interval supports historical legal analysis and effective-date queries.

🗂️
Topic hierarchy

The broaderTopic property enables semantic expansion and faceted navigation over legal taxonomies.
