Legal Knowledge Base Ontology (English)
=======================================

1) Purpose and Scope
--------------------
This ontology models legal knowledge for a Brazilian-oriented legal knowledge base.
It represents laws, legal provisions, court cases, judicial decisions, parties, topics,
and temporal validity so that legal information can be indexed, linked, and queried.

Primary goals:
- Normalize legal entities and relationships.
- Support retrieval (RAG/GraphRAG) with explicit legal structure.
- Enable reasoning over citations, applicability, and legal validity periods.


2) Core Classes
---------------
LegalSource: Generic legal source (norms, decisions, doctrine-like references).
Norm: Normative act (constitution, law, decree, resolution, ordinance).
Provision: Granular legal unit (article, paragraph, inciso, alinea).
Decision: Judicial decision (sentence, ruling, appellate decision).
Case: Judicial process identified by a case number.
Court: Judicial body (for example STF, STJ, TJSP).
Chamber: Panel/chamber inside a court.
Person / Organization: Basic legal actors.
Party: Litigating party in a case (person or organization).
Lawyer: Person subclass representing legal counsel.
Judge: Person subclass representing a judge.
Topic: Legal taxonomy concept (for example Civil Liability, Moral Damages).
Jurisdiction: Territorial/legal scope (for example BR, SP).
TemporalValidity: Effective interval for legal sources.


3) Controlled Type Hierarchies (Enumerations)
----------------------------------------------
NormType subclasses: Constitution, Law, Decree, Resolution, Ordinance.
ProvisionType subclasses: Article, Paragraph, Inciso, Alinea.
DecisionType subclasses: Acordao, Sentenca, Despacho.
PartyRole subclasses: Plaintiff, Defendant, Appellant, Appellee.

These controlled subclasses help standardize values for legal classification.


4) Key Object Properties
------------------------
Norm -> Provision:
- hasProvision / isProvisionOf (inverse relation).

Norm -> Norm:
- amends, revokes, regulates.

Provision -> Provision:
- referencesProvision.

Decision -> LegalSource:
- cites (references norms/provisions/decisions).

Decision -> Provision:
- applies (legal basis applied by the decision).

Decision -> Case:
- decidesCase.

Case -> Court / Chamber / Party:
- heardByCourt, heardByChamber, hasParty.

Party -> Person|Organization / PartyRole / Lawyer:
- partyEntity, partyRole, representedBy.

LegalSource -> Topic / Jurisdiction / TemporalValidity:
- aboutTopic, inJurisdiction, hasValidity.

Topic -> Topic:
- broaderTopic (taxonomy hierarchy).


5) Key Datatype Properties
--------------------------
identifier (string): canonical legal source id.
title (string): legal title.
text (string): legal text content.
publishedOn (date): publication date for norms.
decisionDate (date): date of judicial decision.
caseNumber (string): judicial case identifier.
effectiveFrom / effectiveTo (date): legal validity interval.


6) Structural Constraints
-------------------------
Provision cardinality rule:
- Every Provision belongs to exactly 1 Norm.

Decision cardinality rule:
- Every Decision decides at least 1 Case.

Case cardinality rule:
- Every Case is heard by at least 1 Court.

These constraints keep the graph legally coherent and reduce data ambiguity.


7) Example Legal Chain
----------------------
- Norm: Brazilian Civil Code (Law 10.406/2002).
- Provision: Article 186 (civil liability by wrongful act).
- Case: REsp 123456, heard by STJ.
- Decision: a decision applies Article 186 and cites the Civil Code.
- Topic linkage: Moral Damages -> broader topic Civil Liability.
- Jurisdiction: BR.

This chain enables queries such as:
- Which decisions applied Article 186?
- Which topics are most cited in STJ cases?
- Which provisions are currently effective in BR?


8) Why This Ontology Works Well for Graph RAG
----------------------------------------------
- Entity normalization improves retrieval precision.
- Explicit edges (cites/applies/decidesCase) enable multi-hop reasoning.
- Temporal validity supports historical legal analysis.
- Topic hierarchy enables semantic expansion and faceted navigation.
